BLACK WOMEN, BLACK EXCELLENCE.

Black people have been around for centuries,we have been belittled,stolen from, and discriminated against. But, we have moved regardless.

This page is about celebrating black women.It is about celebrating our culture and our excellence, Black Women Excellence.

Here are 10 black women that are making history in 2020:

    • "Black-ish" star Marsai Martin is the youngest executive producer in history, thanks to 2019 comedy movie "Little".

    • Lizzo, who shot to fame in 2019 for hit single "Truth hurts", was the most nominated artist at the 2020 Grammys.

    • South Sudanese-Australian supermodel Adut Akech Bior-, former child refugee herself- is working with the UN to help refugees.

    • Lena Waithe was the first black woman to win a Primetime Emmy for Outstanding Writing in a Comedy Series, and her latest movie -Queen&Slim- is out in theaters.

    • Rihanna is the world's richest female musician, valued at $600 million. According to Forbes, the singer amassed the fortune after launching wildly popular Fenty beauty makeup line and Savage Fenty lingerie line.

    • The founder of the #MeToo movement, Tarana Burke, continues to campaign against sexual violence.

    • Ari Melenciano is an innovator bringing together art, science, history, and black culture.

    • Dr. Shirley Jackson is the first black woman to lead a top-ranked research university.

    • Dr. Johnnetta Betsch Cole was an honoree at the National Women's History Museum's Women Making History Awards.

    • Misty Copeland is the first black woman to be the American Ballet Theatre's principal dancer.

Every February is "Black History Month" in the U.S.A, a whole month dedicated to educating the young as well as the old about the history of black people, celebrating the black community and remembering all of our black heroes.
